## Purpose / Mission

ChAIDev is a **FUN and OPTIONAL forum for humans who are actively exploring how AI is changing their work and their lives**. While the community is **rooted in engineers, builders, and tinkerers** with deep technical experience — which is the heart of the conversation and the energy we want to preserve — we have to acknowledge that **the line between engineer and non-engineer is blurring fast**. Software is quickly becoming democratized, and we see this as a great thing! So **the door isn't gated by background** — the room is centered around human beings **actively building**, experimenting, shipping, going deep with these tools, and **adjacent voices are very welcome** too. Vibe coders, Claude power users, anyone drawn to that current. Professionally or personally. We share what's working, what's breaking, what's surprising. Loosely modeled on the old SF DevOps meetup, CoffeeOps.

---

## The Vibe

**The energy here isn't get-rich-quick AI strategy or "build me a 100M B2B SaaS, chat!"** It's joy, fun, and excitement at building things at a new pace and magnitude, empowered by these tools. More for the love of the game than for winning the game.

**The conversation should be positive.** It's fun to gripe about Claude or whatever's breaking this week — but if we're all upset all the time about everything, this stops being fun. We aim for adult, constructive engagement: encouragement for people's approaches, real and helpful criticism when it's warranted.

**All voices are encouraged, not just the loudest.** Especially on the live call, watch the airtime — not enough breathing room in the discussion turns the room into an *"I-feel-stupid-after-walking-into-the-bikeshop-for-repairs"* phenomenon instead of a welcome mat. The bar so far has been great; the goal is to keep it that way as the group grows.

## The Slack

The day-to-day life of ChAIDev happens here — the async lifeblood of the community. Channels grow and shift organically over time as conversations and projects emerge. What started as a touchpoint between meetings is now arguably the more valuable component of this thing.

*Currently a work in progress: the Slack lives in Pat's Lightwire LLC workspace, which will be renamed in place (including the URL) to a dedicated ChAIDev workspace soon. `#chai-dev` will remain the main channel.*

## The Meeting

A monthly ~90-minute video call — a way of anchoring the ungrounded pseudo-anonymity of a chatroom in reality on a regular basis. Low pressure, fun, optional. We're all busy people and it's not always easy to make it; that's fine. The meeting is there to put faces and voices to Slack handles you've been chatting with, and to share what you've been building.

The meeting also doubles as a low-stakes sampler for prospective members — folks who want to feel out whether this is the kind of group they'd value being part of can drop in before committing to the full membership ritual (see *Inviting New Members* below).

**Agenda (~90 min):**

1. **Spotlight / Demo** (15–25 min) — usually one participant shows a project or tool. Sometimes open-mic / micro-demo style where several people each share something quick.
2. **Round Table / Topic Discussion** (45–60 min) — themed discussion. Everyone shares experiences, insights, and questions.
3. **Wrap-Up** (5–10 min) — key takeaways, next topic, volunteer for next demo, follow-up links and resources.

We no longer do round-robin intros at the meeting. New member intros happen async in `#chai-dev-n00bs` (see below). The call is for substantive conversation; we trust the room to be full of valuable voices and jump right in.

**Recording policy.** Demos and spotlight presentations are recorded and archived in `#chai-dev-recordings`. The round-table discussion afterward is *not* recorded — that's where we speak openly about works-in-progress, frustrations, and what's actually happening at our jobs.

## Ground Rules

Apply to both the meeting and the Slack.

1. **Secret Rule.** The first rule of ChAI Dev...
2. **Professional Boundaries.** Don't share anything covered by NDAs or business-sensitive information.
3. **Recording Policy.** Demos are recorded and archived. Round-table discussions and Slack threads are not — that's how we keep this honest.
4. **Respect & Collaboration.** Everyone's experience counts, whether full-time or side project. Listen actively, avoid interruptions, encourage curiosity.
5. **Participation.** Everyone is welcome to contribute, ask questions, and share demos or ideas. Lurking is fine too.
6. **Time Awareness.** Stick to time limits in the meeting so everyone gets airtime.

## Inviting New Members

### On Preserving The Spirit of ChAIDev
The way I initially pitched the idea to this founding group of participants was that the idea was to  come together periodically with similar **peers who are working through the growth edges of this new era of AI-driven engineering** to see if we might all **benefit from this manner of inter-org cross pollination**. The **Slack grew up from that** — and while it is now arguably the more important, valuable, and fun part of the community — for now I'd like to **keep the framing** of the group to outsiders / new invitees preserved.

**Not that the Slack is "secret", but almost**.  So when ChAIDev invariably comes up in conversations amongst your own networks, **I'm asking that you frame it as *"a community with a monthly get-together. What's that? Oh yes we also have a Slack."* rather than *"hey, join this rockin' Slack I am a part of."*** As the community grows, I'd like it to remain, conceptually, a community of peers that periodically finds value in getting together to bounce ideas off of each other. Real magic happens in high-fidelity, non-async interactions — and while the Slack may be its own (higher?) value proposition - **for now I would like to keep this original spirit** of gathering together intact - while allowing the async/Slack momentum/value/fun to continue to build.

As such, **while the process has shifted** from *"join a call → Slack invite"* to *"funnel → join the Slack, call is optional"* - please see **this shift as logistical in nature**, not ideological.  Making a monthly 12 noon Friday video call is a crap shoot for our busy modern lives - so we need a better way to join up.  **This new slack-centric process opens up access to the community — but by codifying some of the former intent here, I hope we can preserve the original spirit of ChAIDev for some time yet.**

— Pat

### Guidance

- **Quality over quantity** of membership at this point. We're still (may always be?) in the [inner-ring stage](/community-moderation#2-size-is-design-different-sizes-are-different-products); for now let's be thoughtful about what voices we add to the room.
- **Do invite.** Active builders and tinkerers — engineers, vibe coders, scientists, lawyers, designers — anyone going deep with AI in their work. Adjacent voices going deep with AI in their own way — or who will genuinely get a lot of value out of being immersed here — are welcome too; each invite just leans the room toward or away from that center. Unsure? Let's discuss.
- **Don't invite.** Someone just because they mentioned "I've started using Claude" one time peripherally. We're avoiding filling the room with people who aren't active but are too nice to DELETE.
- **On friction.** We want some friction at the door to provide some signal on future engagement. While that friction mostly lands on you, "the sponsor," to put thought into whether they should move to the next steps, it ultimately requires the candidate to write a short intro post for the Slack. We're erring on the balance of some friction but not onerous at this point.

### The Funnel

New members should be selected/filtered by this funnel. Work through it one step at a time — don't skip any.

1. **Someone you already network with** — personal or professional, someone you're already having these discussions with on the side. Bring people you'd vouch for personally.
2. **You've had a substantive AI-centric workflow or engineering discussion** with them that prompted the idea to bring them in.
3. **Check in with Pat** as community moderator on whether they sound like a good fit — before any outreach about the community.
4. **Have a feeler-check conversation** with them about ChAIDev — gauge whether they'd be into it, get a sense of the energy on their end.
5. **Fork — invite to the monthly call, or bring them in directly?**
   - **Already a strong fit in your mind?** Get Pat's greenlight first, then verbally invite and coach them through the n00bs post.
   - **Want a feeler check?** Invite them to the next monthly call as your guest — just *"come check it out,"* don't bring up Slack yet. If after the call they seem interested, circle back to Pat for a greenlight, then verbally invite and coach the post.
6. **Pat sends the Slack invite** — once the post lands, the new member shows up in their thread and gets welcomed.

### Joining Logistics

The actual gate is a non-rote intro post in `#chai-dev-n00bs`. Membership is decided ahead by the sponsor/Pat; the post isn't a vote or filter.

The new member isn't in Slack yet, so the sponsor posts on their behalf. Once the post lands, Pat sends the Slack invite — the new member shows up in their own thread and gets welcomed.

### The `#chai-dev-n00bs` Post — Pinned Prompt

> **Welcome to `#chai-dev-n00bs`**
>
> To get a ChAIDev Slack invite, post here. We're a curated room and this post is how we get to know you.
>
> Three sections — pick at least one angle from each. Mix and match as more apply; you don't have to hit every option.
>
> Feel free to throw this through your favorite LLM personal assistant / second brain if you have one and it knows enough to generate this post — we care about the content, not the labor.
>
> ---
>
> **Section 1 — Who you are / what you bring**
>
> *Pick at least one of the following — mix and match if more apply:*
>
> - **Your current AI / LLM relationship** — what tools you use, how, what's working, what's frustrating.
> - **How AI has been influencing your work or life** — has your job changed? Are you building differently? Personal impact?
> - **AI projects you're working on** — startups, OSS, side projects, initiatives, art. *Brief* — links encouraged.
> - **AI skepticism** — if you're a thoughtful skeptic, share it. ("AI bad" is not real critique.)
> - **AI-peripheral angle** — non-engineering work where you're using (or not using) AI.
> - **Novel AI thinking** — anything you've been mulling, exploring, or wrestling with.
> - **Recent AI rabbit holes** — papers, tools, projects, art, ideas. What's grabbed you lately?
> - **Skills, expertise, or perspective you can offer back** — what could you help others with?
>
> ---
>
> **Section 2 — What you're hoping for here**
>
> *Pick at least one of the following — mix and match if more apply:*
>
> - **What you're hoping to get out of this kind of networking community** — be specific.
> - **Questions you'd love the community to weigh in on** — survey-style or open-ended.
> - **Specific feedback you're looking for** on something you're working on.
> - **Challenges or blockers you're stuck on.**
> - **Topics you'd want to see in a monthly meetup.**
> - **Collaborations you'd be open to** — hackathons, side-project partners, paper-discussion buddies, advisors.
>
> ---
>
> **Section 3 — Fun fact**
>
> *Pick one (or two if you're feeling generous):*
>
> - A fun fact
> - The most "un-I" thing AI has done for you lately
> - Your hidden talent or hobby
> - A picture of your workspace, pet, yard, pantry, your chai or coffee mug, or something truly random or truly ordinary you're looking at right now
>
> ---
>
> **Format:** We read markdown for a living — headers, bullets, and tables are encouraged. But all forms of prose and poetry are also welcome ;)
>
> **Length:** A few paragraphs of substance for Sections 1 and 2; a few sentences for Section 3. Substantive but not exhausting.
>
> **What happens next:** Once your post is up, Pat will send the Slack invite. Welcome.

\* **On white-gloving.** You're welcome to white-glove it for someone who'd be a real value-add to the room but is literally too busy to write the post themselves — write the intro for them based on their existing public work. Of course, if your new invite turns out to suck we will all judge you quite passive-aggressively... so don't fuck it up!

### Regarding TTL/pruning

While there is not yet an established TTL or pruning process, Pat reserves the right as de facto moderator to check in with members after a month or so — especially if contribution stays light in both the Slack and the meeting. Too many anonymous faces without much engagement starts to feel weird; that's not the room we're building. A more formal approach may develop as the group grows; TBD.
